# Family Economy System

> Separating into three layers so the child doesn't grow up thinking money appears magically, but also doesn't feel like love and family are transactional.

---

## Layer 1: Citizenship (Not Paid)

These are requirements for membership in the family. No bounty. No negotiation. No payment.

### Categories

- **Personal Care** — Brush teeth, bathe, make bed, clean room
- **Respect** — Polite speech, honesty, follow household rules
- **Education** — Attend school, complete homework, read assigned books
- **Family** — Participate in family meals, attend family meetings

**The lesson:** "You do these because you are a member of the family." Not because you get paid.

---

## Layer 2: Contribution Bounties

Payment tied to value creation.

### Bronze Tier (Age 5–8)
Household help tasks at entry-level compensation.

### Silver Tier (Age 8–12)
Property and organization tasks requiring more effort.

### Gold Tier (Age 12–16)
Larger projects requiring sustained effort and some skill.

### Platinum Tier (Age 16+)
Real projects approaching market rates. Examples: website building, tutoring, business assistance, market research, AI assistance, technical projects.

---

## Layer 3: Entrepreneurship

### Opportunity Board

The child proposes projects using this template:

| Element | Question |
|---------|----------|
| Problem | What problem did you notice? |
| Solution | What do you want to do? |
| Cost | What resources needed? |
| Revenue | How will value be created? |
| Risk | What could fail? |
| Reward | How should profit be shared? |

---

## Family Treasury Board

Three buckets:

| Bucket | Purpose | Example Split |
|--------|---------|---------------|
| **Spend** | Immediate use | 50% |
| **Save** | Future purchases | 30% |
| **Invest** | Long-term wealth | 20% |

Adjust with age.

---

## Bounty Eligibility Board

Before someone can accept bounties, they must maintain:
- Honesty
- Respect
- School effort
- Household citizenship

### Progression Levels

| Level | Access |
|-------|--------|
| Apprentice | Bronze bounties |
| Journeyman | Silver bounties |
| Craftsman | Gold bounties |
| Master | Propose projects and negotiate rates |

---

## Reputation System

Instead of punishment, track:

- **Reliability** — Does he finish?
- **Quality** — Was it done well?
- **Initiative** — Did he notice work?
- **Leadership** — Can he coordinate others?

Each scored 1–5. These determine eligibility.

---

## The Real Goal

The progression:

```
Allowance → Bounties → Projects → Entrepreneurship → Stewardship
```

The final lesson isn't "Do chores, get money."

The lesson is:

> **"Value creation creates opportunity. Character determines eligibility. Trust unlocks responsibility. Responsibility unlocks freedom."**

That's much closer to how the adult world actually works.
