# Bounty Framework Board

> If you define specific chores and dollar amounts now, you'll be redesigning the system every few years. What you actually want is a framework that remains valid from age 5 to age 50.

---

## Tier 0: Citizenship

**Not bounties.** Family membership requirements.

### Categories
- Self-care
- Household participation
- Respect
- Education
- Spiritual obligations
- Community participation

---

## Tier 1: Contribution

Work that creates value for others.

### Categories
- Household
- Property
- Family support
- Caregiving
- Organization
- Administration

**Lessons:** Work, Reliability, Completion

---

## Tier 2: Competence

Work requiring developed skill.

### Categories
- Technical
- Creative
- Mechanical
- Academic
- Research
- Digital

**Lessons:** Quality, Skill development, Mastery

---

## Tier 3: Stewardship

Management of assets and responsibilities.

### Categories
- Budgeting
- Planning
- Inventory
- Scheduling
- Resource allocation
- Leadership

**Lessons:** Trust, Accountability, Long-term thinking

---

## Tier 4: Initiative

Finding problems without being asked.

### Categories
- Process improvement
- Opportunity identification
- Innovation
- Service projects
- Community improvement

**Lessons:** Ownership, Vision, Agency

---

## Tier 5: Entrepreneurship

Creating entirely new value.

### Categories
- Products
- Services
- Education
- Technology
- Content
- Ventures

**Lessons:** Risk, Market forces, Negotiation, Value creation

- Initiative

---

## Reward Board

Not all rewards should be money.

| Category | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| **Currency** | Financial compensation |
| **Authority** | More decision-making power |
| **Access** | Tools, resources, privileges |
| **Responsibility** | Greater trust |
| **Knowledge** | Mentorship, training, experiences |
| **Legacy** | Permanent recognition of meaningful accomplishments |

---

## Navigation Connection

This connects into the larger lifetime navigation system:

- **Position** — Current maturity level
- **Azimuth** — What kind of person is the child becoming?
- **Velocity** — How quickly is responsibility increasing?
- **Acceleration** — Is growth itself increasing?
- **Current** — What environmental forces help or hinder progress?

The bounty system becomes one subsystem inside the larger lifetime map rather than a standalone chore chart. It evolves naturally from childhood contributions into adult stewardship, leadership, and eventually mentorship of others.
