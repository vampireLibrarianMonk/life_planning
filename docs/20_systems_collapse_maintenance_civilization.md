# Civic / Science Venture: Systems Collapse & Maintenance Civilization
## Life After People-Style Formation Study Bounty Track

This markdown defines a repeatable bounty track for the Life Planning app inspired by the educational value of a *Life After People*-style docuseries.

The purpose is not to teach apocalypse entertainment. The purpose is to teach that civilization is maintained by hidden labor, institutions, knowledge, infrastructure, stewardship, and repeated human responsibility. When maintenance stops, systems decay.

# 2. Plain-English Purpose

This track teaches the child:

```text
Civilization does not run by itself.

Roads, water systems, power grids, homes, hospitals, farms, records, laws, schools, churches, software, machines, and buildings all require maintenance.

If people stop caring for them, they decay.
```

The child learns that inheritance is not only what they receive. Inheritance is also what they must maintain, repair, or replace.

# 5. What Not To Teach

This track should not become:

- doomsday fantasy
- fear training
- survivalist paranoia
- anti-civilization propaganda
- entertainment-only disaster watching
- contempt for modern systems
- contempt for ordinary workers
- political blame without analysis

The point is not:

```text
Civilization is doomed.
```

The point is:

```text
Civilization is maintained. What we do not maintain eventually fails.
```

# 6. Universal Bounty Statement

```text
Study one system, structure, institution, or episode showing what happens when human maintenance stops. Identify what humans built, what maintenance it required, how it decays, who normally prevents that decay, and what this teaches about stewardship.
```

# 10. Short Bounty Statements

## Episode Study Bounty

```text
Watch one episode or segment about what happens when humans disappear or stop maintaining a system. Create a fact sheet explaining what decays, why it decays, and what humans normally do to prevent it.
```

## Infrastructure Bounty

```text
Choose one piece of infrastructure such as a bridge, road, water system, power line, dam, or sewer system. Explain what it does, how it is maintained, and what happens if it is neglected.
```

## Hidden Labor Bounty

```text
Identify the workers who keep one ordinary system functioning. Explain why people often fail to notice their work until something breaks.
```

## Household Maintenance Bounty

```text
Choose one household system such as plumbing, roofing, HVAC, electrical wiring, or vehicle maintenance. Explain what must be maintained and what happens if the family ignores it.
```

## Civilization Gratitude Bounty

```text
Choose one ordinary comfort such as clean water, electricity, trash pickup, roads, hospitals, or grocery stores. Trace the people and systems that make it possible.
```

## Knowledge Decay Bounty

```text
Choose one type of knowledge that could be lost if not taught, documented, or practiced. Explain what happens when a society forgets how to maintain something important.
```

## Local Infrastructure Walk Bounty

```text
Walk or drive through the local area and identify five systems that require maintenance. Explain who likely maintains them and what would happen if they failed.
```

## Emergency Preparedness Bounty

```text
Choose one system your family depends on. Explain what the family would do if that system failed for one day, one week, or one month.
```

# 15. Final Principle

```text
Civilization is not magic.

It is maintained by people.

If people stop maintaining what they inherit, the world does not stay still.

It decays.
```

The child should learn to ask:

```text
What am I benefiting from that someone else maintained?

What will fail if my generation refuses to maintain it?

What must I repair, preserve, or pass on?
```
