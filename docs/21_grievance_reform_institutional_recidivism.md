# Civic Venture: Grievance, Reform & Institutional Recidivism Research Sheet
## Repeatable Civic Bounty Track for the Life Planning App

This markdown defines a repeatable Civic pillar bounty track for studying current grievances against historical patterns.

The goal is to determine whether a present problem is truly new, recurring, inherited, structurally embedded, exaggerated, exploited, or beyond the ability of current leaders to fix without deeper reform.

This is not a complaint collector.

It is a disciplined civic research sheet.

# 2. Plain-English Purpose

People often hear grievances as slogans.

Examples:

```text
The system is broken.
The people in charge failed.
This has never happened before.
The other side caused this.
Nothing can be fixed.
Burn it down.
Trust the plan.
```

This bounty forces the child to slow down and ask:

```text
Is the grievance real?
Has it happened before?
Who complained before?
What was tried before?
Did reform work?
Did the problem return?
Is this recidivism?
Who benefits from the problem remaining unresolved?
Can current leaders realistically fix it?
What would repair actually require?
```

---

# 3. Core Principle

```text
A complaint is not enough.

A serious citizen asks whether the problem is real, whether it has happened before, what was tried, why it returned, and what kind of repair would actually be required.
```

# 5. Key Question

This sheet should test, not assume, the conclusion.

The key question is:

```text
Is this grievance a new failure, an old failure returning, a permanent tradeoff, a leadership failure, a structural flaw, or a problem being exaggerated for power?
```

---

# 6. Guardrails

## Do Not Teach Resentment

The child should not learn to hoard grievances as identity.

## Do Not Teach Doomerism

The child should not conclude every recurring problem is impossible to repair.

## Do Not Teach Blind Faith in Reformers

The child should not assume new leaders can fix old problems simply by promising reform.

## Do Not Teach Automatic Distrust

The child should not assume all institutions are malicious.

## Do Not Teach Automatic Trust

The child should not assume institutions can fix everything if given enough authority.

## Do Not Teach Political Tribalism

The child should not use the sheet to prove that one side is always guilty.

## Do Not Teach Conspiracy Reflex

The child should not treat recurrence as proof of hidden control without evidence.

# 11. Grievance Classification

Use these categories in the app.

## New Problem

A problem caused by recent changes.

## Recurring Problem

A problem seen before that has returned.

## Structural Problem

A problem built into the incentives or design of the institution.

## Leadership Failure

A problem caused mainly by poor current leadership.

## Cultural Failure

A problem caused by repeated habits, values, expectations, or social behavior.

## Tradeoff Problem

A problem that cannot be fully solved because every option has costs.

## Exploited Problem

A real or partial problem used by others for power, money, attention, or control.

## False or Exaggerated Grievance

A grievance that is unsupported, distorted, or overblown.

## Unclear

Not enough evidence yet.

---

# 12. Source Discipline Categories

The child must separate:

## Evidence

Records, data, direct testimony, official documents, repeated observations, credible investigations.

## Allegation

A claim made by someone but not proven.

## Public Narrative

How people talk about the grievance in public.

## Political Slogan

A simplified phrase meant to persuade or mobilize.

## Historical Fact

Something that can be established from reliable historical sources.

## Pattern

A repeated structure across time.

## Interpretation

A reasoned explanation of what the pattern means.

## Speculation

A possible explanation not yet proven.

## Personal Reaction

What the child feels or thinks, clearly labeled.
