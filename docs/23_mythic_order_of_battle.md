# Repeatable Bounty Entry: Mythic Order of Battle
## Titans, Angels, Demons, Mythology, and Spiritual Pattern Recognition

This markdown is ready for integration into the Life Planning app.

The bounty helps the child study how different traditions describe cosmic conflict, spiritual hierarchy, rebellion, loyalty, divine order, chaos, temptation, heroic struggle, and moral warfare.

This is not meant to make the child obsessed with demons, monsters, war, or mythology.

The goal is to teach pattern recognition:

```text
Who is in the conflict?
What are they fighting over?
What powers or offices do they hold?
What side represents order?
What side represents rebellion, chaos, pride, or corruption?
What lessons should a human being learn?
```

# 3. Child-Facing Bounty Statement

```text
Choose one mythic, religious, spiritual, or legendary conflict. Break down the sides like a simple order of battle. Identify who is involved, what each side wants, who leads, what powers or roles they have, what caused the conflict, how the conflict ends, and what lesson it teaches about order, rebellion, pride, loyalty, sacrifice, temptation, courage, or wisdom.
```

---

# 4. Parent-Facing Purpose

This bounty teaches the child to compare stories of cosmic or spiritual conflict without treating every tradition as identical.

The child learns:

```text
Catholic angels and demons are not the same as Greek gods and Titans.
Greek myth is not Catholic doctrine.
Mythology can still teach patterns.
Religious claims require extra care.
Stories of rebellion, pride, sacrifice, and restoration appear across cultures.
A person should study these stories with curiosity, discipline, and moral caution.
```

# 6. Core Guardrails

## Do Not Treat All Traditions as the Same

```text
Catholic doctrine, Greek myth, Norse myth, fiction, and modern fantasy are different kinds of sources.
```

## Do Not Obsess Over Evil

```text
Study evil to recognize lies and disorder, not to admire it.
```

## Do Not Make a Demon Pokedex

```text
The point is not to collect scary names.

The point is to identify patterns of temptation, pride, rebellion, deception, despair, violence, and disorder.
```

## Do Not Turn Formation Into War Indoctrination

```text
The child is not being trained to hate people.

The enemy is sin, deception, disorder, pride, despair, and evil — not ordinary human beings.
```

# 9. Example Topic: Titanomachy

## Source Type

```text
Greek mythology
```

## Simple Summary

The Titanomachy is the Greek mythic war between the older Titans and the younger Olympian gods. Cronus ruled after overthrowing his father Uranus. Zeus, one of Cronus's children, eventually led a revolt against Cronus and the Titans. The Olympians won and established a new divine order.

## Simple Order of Battle

```text
Side A: Olympians
Leader: Zeus
Allies: Poseidon, Hades, Hera, Hestia, Demeter, Hecatoncheires, Cyclopes
Goal: Overthrow Cronus and establish a new order
Pattern: rebellion against tyranny, succession, new rule, order after conflict

Side B: Titans
Leader: Cronus
Allies: many Titans
Goal: Preserve older rule
Pattern: fear of being overthrown, control, succession conflict
```

## Human Lesson

```text
Power held through fear creates rebellion.

A new order may defeat an old order, but it still must prove whether it is just.
```

## Catholic Guardrail

```text
This is Greek mythology, not Catholic doctrine.

It can teach patterns about pride, succession, fear, and power, but it should not be treated as a revealed account of angels, demons, or God.
```

# 10. Example Topic: Catholic Angels and Demons

## Source Type

```text
Catholic doctrine and Christian tradition
```

## Simple Summary

Catholic teaching holds that angels are spiritual creatures created by God. Some angels rejected God through their own free choice and became demons. Demons are not equal to God. They are creatures. Their power is limited. Their aim is to turn human beings away from God through temptation, deception, accusation, despair, and disorder.

## Simple Order of Battle

```text
Side A: Faithful Angels
Highest loyalty: God
Known figures: St. Michael, St. Gabriel, St. Raphael
Goal: Serve God, protect, announce, guide, worship
Pattern: obedience, humility, service, protection, truth

Side B: Fallen Angels / Demons
Leader: Satan / the devil
Goal: rebellion against God, temptation of human beings, accusation, deception, despair
Pattern: pride, envy, deception, division, false freedom, hatred of God
```

## Human Lesson

```text
The spiritual battle is not about hating other people.

It is about resisting lies, sin, despair, pride, and anything that pulls the soul away from God.
```

## Catholic Guardrail

```text
Do not study demons to become fascinated by demons.

Study demonic patterns to recognize lies, resist sin, preserve freedom, and return to God.
```

# 11. Example Topic: St. Michael and the Dragon

## Source Type

```text
Scripture / Christian tradition
```

## Simple Summary

The Book of Revelation describes Michael and his angels fighting the dragon. The dragon is identified with the devil and Satan. Michael represents faithful angelic resistance to evil. The story is often used in Catholic devotion as a symbol of spiritual warfare and protection.

## Simple Order of Battle

```text
Side A: Michael and his angels
Goal: faithful resistance to evil
Pattern: courage, obedience, protection, service to God

Side B: Dragon / Satan and his angels
Goal: rebellion, accusation, deception
Pattern: pride, hatred, disorder, accusation
```

## Human Lesson

```text
Courage is not rage.

Spiritual defense means standing with truth and God against deception and evil.
```

# 12. Source Quality Rules

For each bounty, classify sources.

```text
Level 1: Primary sacred text or original ancient source
Level 2: Official Church teaching or serious scholarly source
Level 3: Encyclopedia / educational source
Level 4: Retelling, documentary, podcast, or popular summary
Level 5: Fiction, fan theory, speculation, or internet commentary
```

App rule:

```text
The lower the source quality, the more careful the conclusion must be.
```
