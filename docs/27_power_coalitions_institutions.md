# Pillar: Power, Coalitions & Institutions

## Vision
Develop the lifelong ability to understand how organized groups gain, exercise, maintain, share, and lose influence.

## Guiding Principle
Every enduring organization creates boundaries, forms coalitions, establishes priorities, and maintains formal or informal lists of who belongs, who leads, who is trusted, and who is excluded.

## Purpose
Teach the learner to analyze organizations by asking:
- How was it formed?
- What gives it legitimacy?
- Who benefits?
- Who is excluded?
- What coalitions sustain it?
- What causes fragmentation or realignment?

## Boundary & Membership Systems

Map every organization using common categories:

- Membership
- Leadership
- Authority
- Trust
- Coalition Partners
- Opposition
- Recruitment
- Exclusion
- Temporary Exceptions
- Priority Ranking
- Succession

## Coalition Tension Tracker

For every organization evaluate:
- Current allies
- Current opponents
- Sources of votes
- Sources of funding
- Sources of legitimacy
- Internal disagreements
- Future realignment risks

## Means vs. Ends

Document:
- Immediate objectives
- Long-term vision
- Present compromises
- Future implications

Question:
Are today's allies compatible with tomorrow's vision?

## Repeatable Bounty

Analyze one institution, movement, company, religion, nation, or coalition.

Record:
- Origin
- Mission
- Legitimacy
- Organizational Lists
- Coalition Map
- Priority Hierarchy
- Current Tensions
- Future Scenarios
- Lessons Learned

## Guardrails

- Separate official policy from informal practice.
- Distinguish analysis from endorsement.
- Avoid oversimplification.
- Expect organizations to evolve over time.
