## Image Summary and Source Notes: “The People Don’t Know Their True Power”

This political cartoon shows a politician or public speaker standing behind a lectern at the far end of a wooden plank extending over the edge of a cliff. The opposite end of the plank rests safely on solid ground and is being weighed down by a crowd of people. The crowd appears to be listening to the politician; some people hold signs or placards. One person near the back of the crowd is stepping off the plank, implying that the crowd may not realize it is physically supporting the politician’s position.

The caption at the top reads: “THE PEOPLE DON’T KNOW THEIR TRUE POWER.”

The cartoon’s central metaphor is that political authority depends on the consent, participation, obedience, or support of the governed. The leader appears powerful, but the image shows that his position is structurally dependent on the people beneath him. If enough people withdraw their support, the leader loses balance and falls.
