# Lesson Plan: All 7 Dimensions Explained in Detail (From 0D to Infinity)

## Source
YouTube — https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-gPFxMHWV8w

## Pillar
Dimensional Navigation / Scientific Reality Testing

## Focus Questions
1. What is a dimension in the simplest sense?
2. How does perception change as you move from one dimension to the next?
3. What happens to a creature that can only perceive one dimension lower than it exists in?
4. Why does the video say the brain "shuts down" at higher dimensions?
5. How does this connect to the Life Plan's concept of dimensional thinking — that some consequences are invisible from lower perspectives?

## Key Concepts to Identify
- Degrees of freedom (0D: point, 1D: line, 2D: plane, 3D: volume, 4D: time/tesseract)
- Perception is always one dimension lower than existence
- Flatland analogy — creatures can't perceive dimensions above their own
- Higher dimensions as metaphor for levels of reality we cannot directly see
- The connection between mathematical dimensions and formation thinking (what you can't see from your current position still affects you)

## Goals
- **Rebrief:** Explain each dimension (0-7) in your own words and what it would feel like to exist there.
- **Expansion:** Connect the concept of "perceiving one dimension lower" to how people often only see immediate consequences (1D thinking) instead of long-term, multi-factor consequences (higher-dimensional thinking). How does this relate to the Life Plan's dimensional navigation pillar?
- **Teachback:** Explain to a family member how a 2D creature would perceive a 3D sphere passing through its plane. Then explain how this might be a metaphor for things humans experience but can't fully comprehend.
