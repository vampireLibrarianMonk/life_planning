# Lesson Plan: Stop Trying So Hard — The Secret of John 15 Explained Simply

## Source
YouTube — https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pGoGBeb5AcM

## Pillar
Catholic Formation / Spiritual

## Focus Questions
1. What does "abide" mean in John 15?
2. What is the difference between trying harder and abiding?
3. What does the vine-and-branches metaphor teach about where growth actually comes from?
4. Why does Jesus say "apart from me you can do nothing" — and what does that mean practically, not just theologically?
5. How does this connect to the Life Plan's principle that formation is not self-construction but remaining connected to something greater?

## Key Concepts to Identify
- Abide (Greek: menō) — to remain, stay, dwell
- The vine and branches metaphor (John 15:1-8)
- Fruit comes from connection, not effort alone
- Pruning — God removes what is unproductive AND cuts back what IS productive to make it more fruitful
- The difference between spiritual exhaustion (trying to produce fruit by willpower) and spiritual rest (remaining connected and letting growth happen through you)
- "Apart from me you can do nothing" — not laziness, but proper source of strength

## Goals
- **Rebrief:** Explain the vine-and-branches metaphor in your own words. What is the vine? What are the branches? What is the fruit? What does the gardener do?
- **Expansion:** Think about one area of your life where you have been "trying harder" but feeling stuck. How would "abiding" look different from "trying"? What's the difference between giving up and resting in connection? Connect to one other pillar (character, resilience, dimensional navigation).
- **Teachback:** Explain to a family member what John 15 teaches about where real growth comes from. Use a real example from your own life where effort without connection failed, or where staying connected produced unexpected fruit.
