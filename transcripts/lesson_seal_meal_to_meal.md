# Lesson Plan: Ex Navy SEAL — Surviving Hell Week (Meal to Meal)

## Source
YouTube — https://www.youtube.com/shorts/ (Navy SEAL BUD/S survival technique)

## Pillar
Resilience / Life Navigation

## Focus Questions
1. What is the "meal to meal" technique and why does it work?
2. Why does breaking an impossible challenge into small survivable segments make it bearable?
3. What is the difference between thinking about the whole problem vs. thinking about the next checkpoint?
4. How does this connect to the Life Navigation pillar's concept of course correction and horizons of perception?
5. Where in your life right now could you apply "just get to the next meal" thinking?

## Key Concepts to Identify
- Segmenting: breaking an unbearable stretch into survivable chunks
- The next checkpoint is always reachable even when the finish line is not
- Daily ritual of acknowledgment: "Day X and I'm still here"
- The system doesn't need you to feel motivated, it needs you to reach the next meal
- This is not inspiration. This is operational technique for endurance under extreme stress.

## Goals
- **Rebrief:** Explain the meal-to-meal technique in your own words. Why does the SEAL not think about graduation day? What does he think about instead?
- **Expansion:** Identify one situation in your life (school, training, a hard project, a family difficulty) where the whole problem feels impossible but the next checkpoint is reachable. Design your own "meals" (what are your natural reset points in that challenge?). Connect to the Life Navigation concept: when the horizon is too far, shorten it.
- **Teachback:** Explain to a family member why "just get through today" is not the same as giving up. What is the difference between lowering your standards and narrowing your focus? Teach them the technique and ask: where could they use it?
